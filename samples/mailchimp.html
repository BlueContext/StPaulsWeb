Musical Notes

by Dr. Ben Carlisle, Music Director

This Sunday, one of our hymns (both words and music) will be by our own William Ross!  After studying at Trinity University, Julliard, and the University of Michigan, he enjoyed a long and fruitful career, ultimately retiring after serving at Christ Lutheran Church in San Antonio. We are grateful to Bill for his support of music at St. Paul's.  I am grateful, too, to all the people of St. Paul's, whose love and support of music make it a place that seems to be attractive to retired musicians looking for a church home.

At the Offertory this Sunday, the summer choir sings a setting of "What Wondrous Love Is This?" by living American composer Carson Cooman. In addition to being a prolific composer and supporter of other living composers (check out his YouTube page for probably thousands of recordings of organ pieces by living composers!), Cooman is Composer-In-Residence at Harvard Memorial Church.

Just a reminder: it's still, through August, summer choir season.  That means that any interested singer may join the choir any Sunday at 9:30 to learn the music and sing for the 10:30 service that morning (except for the 17th when we celebrate Back to School and the St. Paul's School's 20th anniversary).  Please contact Ben if you would like more information.  

The St. Paul's Choir will begin Wednesday evening rehearsals on August 20th in preparation for a September return.  Watch this space for more information in the coming weeks!

Summer Redecorating!


We're making some small changes to our campus as we prepare our campus for the new program year. These changes will help our spaces look clean, attractive, and make sure that the furniture we use is meeting our needs. This is a work-in-progress, and we appreciate your patience through our redecorating!

In the meantime, check out the new chairs and tables in the Library! We're grateful for a generous donation from a St. Paul's family that made this possible.

A Collect for St. Paul's

Each month, a different vestry member is writing a prayer for our community.

Let us pray:

Almighty and unchanging God, whose justice endures forever: Guide us by your eternal justice and may it reflect in our lives, that in serving others we may serve you, and through our service to others may we find refuge in your mercy and goodness, and come to know that true hope which is found in you; through Jesus Christ our Lord.  Amen.
